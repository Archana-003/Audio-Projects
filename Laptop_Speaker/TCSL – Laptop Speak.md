**Project 1 : VVDN TCSL – Laptop Speaker Testing**



The following acoustic tests were performed on the laptop speakers for both the Left (L) and Right (R) channels:



Frequency Response measurement for both channels



THD Normalized and Rub \& Buzz tests



Phase Response measurement for both channels



Impedance measurement and verification of resonance frequency



Verification of average sensitivity



**Internal Audio Playback Test**



The internal laptop audio playback was enabled, and the following test conditions were used:



**Test Conditions:**



Distance: 0.5 meter – Rear axis / Off-axis position



Distance: 0.5 meter – On-axis position



**Speaker Driven Test using External Hardware (SoundCheck)**



The speakers were also tested using external hardware through the SoundCheck system.



Test Conditions:



Distance: 0.5 meter – Rear axis / Off-axis position



Distance: 0.5 meter – On-axis position



**Speaker Polarity**



Right Channel (R): + / −



Left Channel (L): + / −



**Issues Observed During Testing**



A speaker polarity change was identified during the phase response test.



The frequency response varies when the speaker placement position is not correct.



For accurate measurement, the Left and Right speakers must be placed properly on the table during testing.



**Key Learnings**



The frequency response of the Left and Right channels should closely match each other.



Stable connections must be ensured throughout the testing process.



The phase response of L and R channels should follow each other, and the LR phase response should remain centered.



The THD curve should remain flat after 1 kHz, indicating stable distortion performance.
