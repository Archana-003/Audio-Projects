What is sound?
Mechanical vibrations cause air molecules in the medium to move back and forth, creating pressure waves, which are perceived by the human ear as sound.

A vibrating object (speaker, string) pushes and pulls air molecules
Air molecules form:
Compression (high pressure)
Rarefaction (low pressure)
These pressure variations travel through air
The ear detects these variations
The brain interprets them as sound


Fundamental and Harmonics:

Fundamental Signal have energy level at one frequency and Harmonics is the multiple of fundamental frequency that is caused due to system nonlinearity.

THD: THD quantify the unwanted harmonics added to the original signal. THD should less that <0.1 %

Stereo balance :Stereo balance is achieved when the left and right audio channels have equal level (amplitude) and matched phase and timing (delay), resulting in a stable stereo balance

Resonance Frequency : The frequency at which a speaker vibrates most efficiently with minimum input power is called the resonant frequency, denoted as Fs.

Sound Pressure Level (SPL) indicates the loudness of sound and is a measure of sound pressure relative to a reference level. It is expressed in decibels (dB), based on sound pressure measured in pascals (Pa).

Impedance is the opposition offered to alternating current (AC). In a loudspeaker, impedance becomes maximum at the resonant frequency because cone motion is maximum, generating a large back EMF in the voice coil, which opposes the applied current and controls the speaker motion.

Dynamic range = Loudest clean sound − Quietest audible sound. Diffrence between two db levels.How the sounds travel in all db level
CD audio (16-bit):
Dynamic range = 96 dB
Very clean music
Cheap phone recording:

Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) tells how strong the desired (original) signal is compared to the background noise.
High SNR - clear sound, very little noise
Low SNR - noisy sound, hiss is audible

Phase describes the time alignment of a signal relative to another signal or a reference.
Amplitude and frequency carry the audio information
Phase affects how signals combine and how sound is perceived (imaging, clarity)
